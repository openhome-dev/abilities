# Panic/Emergency Protocol

A critical safety Ability for OpenHome that integrates with the Twilio Voice API. It features two distinct pathways to call emergency contacts in distress situations: a covert silent alarm for security threats, and a conversational guidance protocol for medical, fire, or other urgent emergencies.

---

## 🌟 Why is it useful?
Normal voice assistants react with conversational replies or simple alarms when you mention danger, which is useless in a real crisis. The **Emergency Panic Protocol** turns OpenHome into an active home responder. Having two distinct emergency pathways is essential:

1.  **Covert Security Pathway (Path A):** In situations like a home robbery, burglary, or domestic threat, announcing that you are calling for help can put you in immediate danger. By using standard conversational codewords, you can trigger a distress call without the intruder knowing you've called for help.
2.  **Interactive Guidance Pathway (Path B):** In non-covert situations (like a house fire, a serious medical fall, or a gas leak), you need the assistant to actively engage with you—providing immediate safety tips and keeping the channel open for additional instructions while you wait for responders.

---

## 🧭 The Two Pathways

### Path A: Silent Alarm (Covert Mode)

#### Detailed Use Case
An intruder enters the home or you feel threatened by someone standing directly next to you. If you shout "Call the police" or "Emergency!", the intruder may become violent or damage the device. Instead, you say a completely normal smart-home command like *"turn off the kitchen lamp"* or a simple question like *"did you feed the dog?"*. 

The assistant processes this trigger, places the Twilio call silently in the background, and shuts down the capability without saying a word. The intruder remains completely unaware that help has been dispatched.

#### Covert Trigger Phrases
`"check the back yard"`, `"turn off the kitchen lamp"`, `"did you feed the dog"`, or `"red flower"`.

#### Covert Demo Transcript
> **User:** *"Did you feed the dog?"*  
> *(Capability Triggered)*  
> 📞 **Twilio Action:** `[Call initiated to Emergency Number in background]`  
> **Speaker Audio:** `[COMPLETE SILENCE]`  
> *(Capability resumes normal flow silently)*
>
> **Distress call received by contact:**  
> *"Emergency Alert! A silent distress call has been initiated from the residence. I repeat, a silent distress call was initiated from the residence. Please dispatch emergency help immediately."*

---

### Path B: Interactive Alarm (Guidance Mode)

#### Detailed Use Case
A grease fire breaks out in your kitchen, or someone falls down the stairs and is injured. You can speak freely, but you are panicked and need immediate, clear advice. You call out *"emergency call"* or *"code red"*. 

The agent immediately prompts you for details. Once you state the problem, it dispatches the call with your details repeated clearly to your contact, reads 3 short, critical safety tips generated by the LLM for that exact scenario, and enters a conversational help loop to guide you through evacuation or first aid until you tell the agent to exit.

#### Interactive Trigger Phrases
`"code red"`, `"start panic protocol"`, `"start emergency protocol"`, or `"emergency call"`.

#### Interactive Demo Transcript
> **User:** *"Emergency call!"*  
> *(Capability Triggered)*  
> **Speaker:** *"Emergency Protocol Active. Initiating panic sequence. Please state the exact nature of your emergency, or say cancel to abort."*  
> **User:** *"A fire broke out in the stove and smoke is filling the kitchen!"*  
> 📞 **Twilio Action:** `[Call initiated to Emergency Number in background]`  
> **Speaker:** *"Call request sent to emergency contacts. While help is on the way, please follow these safety measures: Stay low to avoid smoke inhalation. Close doors to contain fire. Call emergency services immediately."*  
> **Speaker:** *"Do you need help evacuating your home?"*  
> **User:** *"Yes, help me get out!"*  
> **Speaker:** *"Head directly to the nearest exit and stay below the smoke line. I have confirmed your request. Is there any other help you need, or should we exit?"*  
> **User:** *"No, I'm going out now."*  
> **Speaker:** *"Understood. Stay safe. Resuming normal agent flow. Goodbye."*  
> *(Capability exits)*
>
> **Distress call received by contact:**  
> *"Emergency Alert! A panic protocol has been activated at the residence. The resident reports: A fire broke out in the stove and smoke is filling the kitchen!. I repeat, a panic protocol has been activated. The resident reports: A fire broke out in the stove and smoke is filling the kitchen!. Please dispatch emergency help immediately."*

## 🔑 Required API Keys & Setup

To secure your credentials, this Ability uses OpenHome's built-in secure credential store. **Never hardcode secrets in the Ability file.**

1.  Log in to your **OpenHome Dashboard**.
2.  Navigate to **Settings -> API Keys** (under the left sidebar menu or your profile settings).
3.  Scroll down to **Third Party Keys** and click **+ Add** to create the following 4 keys (fill in both the value and `https://twilio.com` as the provider link):
    *   `twilio_account_sid` (Your Twilio Account SID, starts with `AC`)
    *   `twilio_auth_token` (Your Twilio secret Auth Token)
    *   `twilio_phone_number` (Your Twilio phone number, starting with `+`)
    *   `emergency_contact_number` (The contact/dispatch phone number to call, starting with `+`)
4.  Click the green **Save** button on each card.
5.  Go to **My Abilities -> Edit panicprotocol** -> scroll to **Third Party API Keys** -> select/highlight the 4 key badges so they turn cyan, and click **Save Ability**.
