## What It Does
A voice-controlled debate partner that takes the opposing side on any topic you choose. Features structured 3-round debates with real-time scoring, adaptive difficulty, multi-voice delivery, and constructive feedback to help you sharpen your argumentation skills.

## Suggested Trigger Words
- debate
- let's debate
- start a debate
- debate me
- argue with me
- challenge me

## Setup
- **No API key or external service required.** Uses OpenHome's built-in LLM and TTS.
- The opponent speaks in a distinct British voice so you can clearly tell who's talking.

## How It Works
1. User triggers with "let's debate" (or similar)
2. Choose a topic or say "surprise me" for a random one
3. Pick your side (A or B)
4. Select difficulty: Easy, Medium, or Hard
5. Debate across 3 rounds: Opening Statements, Rebuttals, Closing Arguments
6. Receive real-time scores after each round
7. Get a final verdict with detailed feedback and your lifetime win/loss record
8. Play again or exit

## Features
- **Multi-Voice Debate** — The opponent uses a distinct British voice; the moderator uses the default agent voice
- **3-Round Structure** — Opening Statements, Rebuttals, Closing Arguments
- **Adaptive Difficulty** — Easy (gentle), Medium (fair challenge), Hard (no mercy, expert rhetoric)
- **Real-Time Scoring** — Each round scored on Logic, Evidence, Persuasion, and Rebuttal Quality (1-10 each)
- **Constructive Feedback** — Post-debate analysis highlights your strongest moment
- **Lifetime Stats** — Tracks your wins, losses, and draws across all sessions
- **Topic Suggestions** — Pick your own or choose from curated debate topics
- **Replay Support** — Start a new debate or rematch immediately after finishing

## Scoring Rubric
Each round is scored on four criteria (1-10 each):
- **Logic & Reasoning** — Is the argument logically sound?
- **Evidence & Examples** — Are concrete examples used effectively?
- **Persuasiveness** — How compelling is the delivery?
- **Rebuttal Quality** — How well were the opponent's points addressed? (Round 2+ only)

## Difficulty Levels
- **Easy** — Opponent makes decent but surface-level arguments, occasionally misses counterpoints
- **Medium** — Opponent is well-structured and challenging, but fair. Occasionally concedes good points
- **Hard** — Opponent uses advanced techniques: steel-manning, reductio ad absurdum, real-world data. Ruthless

## Example Conversation

**User:** let's debate
**Moderator:** Welcome to Debate Partner! I'll take the opposing side on any topic you choose, and we'll go head to head in a 3-round debate. Give me a topic, or pick from these: Should AI replace teachers in schools? Is social media doing more harm than good? ... Or say 'surprise me' for a random topic.

**User:** Is social media doing more harm than good?
**Moderator:** Nice pick! The topic is: Is social media doing more harm than good? Here are the two sides. Side A: Social media is doing more harm than good... Side B: Social media is a net positive... Which side do you want to argue? Say A or B.

**User:** A
**Moderator:** Got it. You'll argue that social media is harmful. Your opponent will argue it's beneficial. How tough do you want me to be? Easy, medium, or hard?

**User:** hard
**Moderator:** Difficulty set to Hard — no mercy. The debate has 3 rounds. You go first each round. Let's begin! Round 1: Opening Statements. Make your opening argument — I'm listening.

**User:** Social media is destroying mental health, spreading misinformation, and creating echo chambers...
**Moderator:** Interesting point. Here's my response.
**Opponent (British voice):** While concerns about mental health are valid, the data tells a more nuanced story. Social media has democratized information access for billions...

**User:** stop
**Moderator:** Debate ended early. You can start a new debate anytime!
